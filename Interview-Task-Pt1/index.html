2K Interview (Part 1): Marketing Data Analysis

Exploring customer signups, demographics, and purchasing patterns to inform marketing strategy.

Project Context

Part 1 of multi-stage interview process

Given a dataset of customer registrations and purchase behavior, I was tasked with identifying trends, segmenting the customer base, and uncovering actionable insights to inform marketing decisions.

Key Analysis Areas

  • Signup Trends: Understanding how customer registrations evolve over time
  • Demographics: Profiling age distribution of customers
  • Behavior Patterns: Linking first purchase profit to repeat purchase likelihood
  • Customer Segmentation: Using K-Means clustering to identify key customer groups

Findings

The analysis revealed seasonal signup spikes aligned with major promotions, a predominantly mid-20s to early-30s customer base, and clear high-value customer segments. Linear regression indicated a positive relationship between initial order profit and repeat purchase count, while K-Means clustering highlighted three distinct segments: One-Time Shoppers, Loyal Repeat Buyers, and High-Value Customers.
